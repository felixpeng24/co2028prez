## Email Design System Rules

### Sign-off
- Always sign off with **both** co-presidents:
  - Felix Peng
  - Ashlyn Green
- Title: "Class of 2028 Co-Presidents"

### Subject Line
- Format: `[CO 2028] CRAZY TEXT HERE`
- Keep it fun, loud, and exciting

### Styling
- Use inline styles for email HTML (for pasting into converters/Gmail)
- NO buttons, NO section-label underlines, NO info boxes — these look AI-generated
- Mix up colors freely — use red, orange, green, gold, not just the brand palette. Emails should feel colorful and human, not monotone
- Use yellow/gold background highlights on key words (like app names) — e.g. `background: #fff3b0; padding: 2px 6px;`
- Vary font sizes — big bold headlines, smaller subheads, normal body. Create visual contrast
- Links should just be inline colored text, not styled buttons
- Bold key info generously
- Sign-off should have fun nicknames in parentheses with colored text, matching the original email vibe

### Tone
- Casual, hyped, fun — like a group chat announcement
- Emojis welcome but use them naturally, don't force one into every line
- Never corporate or formal
- Should look like a real person typed it in Gmail, not like a designed newsletter

### Colors (use freely, not just these)
- Red/Coral: for hype headers and attention-grabbers
- Orange: for sub-headers and warm accents
- Green/Sage: for section headers (#9fb798 or brighter)
- Navy: #28587b for names in sign-off
- Gold/Yellow highlight: #fff3b0 for key word backgrounds
- Mix in whatever colors feel right — the goal is colorful and fun, not brand-consistent
